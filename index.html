About Valle de Bravo

Valle de Bravo is a picturesque town located in the State of Mexico. Known for its colonial architecture, vibrant lake, and adventure sports like paragliding, it is a popular destination for both relaxation and exploration.

History

Valle de Bravo was founded in 1530 by Franciscan friars. Its colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and temples reflect centuries of tradition. The Parroquia de San Francisco de Asís, completed in 1994, is the tallest church in the State of Mexico.

Main Activities

  • Paragliding: Famous flights from Monte Alto.
  • Boat rides: Relaxing tours on Lake Avándaro.
  • Hiking & cycling: Trails through forests and mountains.
  • Monarch butterfly sanctuary: Seasonal visits from November to March.

Culture & Handicrafts

Local artisans create unique products such as ceramics, embroidered textiles, and wooden furniture. Supporting local crafts means preserving generations of tradition.

Typical Gastronomy

  • Trout with garlic — fresh from the lake.
  • Tamales de charal — made with small local fish.
  • Atole de guayaba — a traditional hot drink.

How to Get There

Valle de Bravo is located 156 km southwest of Mexico City. You can arrive by car in about 2.5 hours or take a bus from the Poniente Terminal.
